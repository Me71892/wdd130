Great Basin National Park Campgrounds

“Great Basin National Park’s high desert rises up to some of the state’s tallest peaks, which harbor the state’s only remaining glacier, cascading mountain streams, alpine lakes, the ancient bristlecone pine (which can live well beyond 4,000 years), and the state’s largest and most spectacular cave system.” (Travel Nevada) Wheeler peak stands at 13,064 feet and is Nevada’s third-tallest mountain. The Great Basin National Park has a lot of diverse plant and animal life. It is also where many campers go to stargaze. The park is known as an "International Dark Sky Park," a great place for gazing at the stars. Watch out though! There are mountain lions about the park. Make sure you bring bear spray as protection in case one attacks. The closest town Baker doesn’t have very many resources so be sure to bring what you need! Camping here is amazing!
