Great Basin National Park Campgrounds

The Great Basin National Park is a high desert that has some of the state’s tallest peaks, which hold the state of Nevada’s glacier, cascading mountain streams, alpine lakes, the ancient bristlecone pine forest(which can live 4,000 years plus), and the state’s largest and cave system, Lehman Caves. Wheeler peak stands at 13,064 feet and is Nevada’s third-tallest mountain. The Great Basin National Park has a lot of diverse plant and animal life. It is also where many campers go to stargaze. The park is known as an International Dark Sky Park, a great place for gazing at the stars. Watch out though there are mountain lions about the park. Make sure you bring your bear spray as protection in case one attacks. The closest town Baker doesn’t have very many resources so be sure to bring what you need! Camping here is amazing!
